Shade Clock

Overview

This p5.js sketch is a sundial clock where the building's shadow acts as the hour hand. It uses the shadow of the Brooklyn Tower, a recently constructed 1,067-foot skyscraper in Downtown Brooklyn, to mark time throughout the day. Like many super-tall luxury buildings in New York, this tower casts dramatic shadows over surrounding homes, parks, and schools from sunrise to sunset.

I recreated the tower's shadow patterns on one mid-October day using shadow-mapping tools that track how sunlight moves across neighborhoods. The project critiques how these structures diminish quality of life for residents by literally stealing their sunlight, questioning why such buildings are permitted when they clearly prioritize profit over community wellbeing.

Note: This clock displays the actual current time in Brooklyn, NY, so it's best viewed during daylight hours. At night, the sketch is intentionally darkened to reflect the time of day.
